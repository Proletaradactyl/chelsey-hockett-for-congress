Chelsey Hockett

01

A Working Family Background.

I grew up in a house where hard work wasn't just discussed, it was lived every day. My family knew what it meant to budget carefully, show up for neighbors, and fight for what's fair. That foundation shaped everything I believe about what government should do for its people.

I'm a wife and mother raising two wonderful kids. I know what it's like to be up at night because you're worried about the cost of groceries, whether your kids' school has enough resources, and what happens if someone in your family gets sick.

02

Why She's Running.

I got tired of watching politicians in Washington talk about working families while taking checks from the corporations that don't have our best interests. I got tired of watching our kids' schools fall behind while billionaires get tax breaks. I got tired of everything being unaffordable in the country my family has worked so hard to build a life in.

I'm running for Congress because I believe you deserve someone who is actually living the struggles they're talking about, not just reading about them in a briefing document. I've been pregnant and uninsured. I've stood in line at the grocery store doing math in my head. I know what this district needs because I need it too.

"I'm running because you deserve someone in Congress who knows what a grocery bill feels like. Someone who isn't beholden to donors, only to their constituents."
— Chelsey Hockett
03

Rooted in This Community.

I've been involved with my community long before I decided to run for office. I've sat with parents at school board meetings, stood alongside workers fighting for fair wages, and organized neighbors who felt like nobody in power was listening to them.

I've marched with friends, supported local businesses, and shown up when it matters all year, not just on the campaign trail.

04

What She Believes.

I believe in an America where if you work full-time, you can afford to live. Where your kids get a well-funded education regardless of your zip code. Where getting sick doesn't mean going bankrupt. Where the government works for people, not corporations.

I'm raising my kids to understand the true history of this country, to find passion, to appreciate past hardships, and to believe that standing up for others should come from absolute kindness, not obligation. That's the kind of America I'm fighting for in Congress.
