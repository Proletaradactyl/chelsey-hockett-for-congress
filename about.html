A Working-Class Background.

Working hard has always been important to me and my family. [We] knew what it meant to budget carefully, show up for neighbors, and fight for what's fair. That foundation shaped everything I believe about what a government should do for its people.

Unlike some other politicians who have sold out to corporations and get rich off of kickbacks, I face the same struggles y'all face: I know what it's like to be up at night because you're worried about the cost of groceries, whether your kids' school has enough resources, and what happens if someone in your family gets sick.

Why I'm Running.

I am sick of the corruption in Washington. Officeholders are supposed to be public servants that provide safety for their communities. But our elected officials are taking money from the very companies they are supposed to be protecting us from!

"I'm running because you deserve someone in Congress who knows what a grocery bill feels like. Someone who isn't beholden to donors, only to their constituents."
— Chelsey Hockett

Rooted in This Community.

I didn't wake up one day and decide to care about this community because of a campaign. I've lived here. Raised my family here. Worked here. Struggled here.

I'm a mom in Terrell who doesn't want to keep watching families work harder every year, just to fall further behind. I've spent years talking with neighbors about rising costs, overcrowded schools, healthcare, wages, housing, and what it feels like to be ignored by the people who are supposed to represent us.

I've shown up at protests, community events, grand openings, small local businesses. I've organized with other everyday people who were tired of feeling powerless and wanted to build something better for East Texans and Dallas residents (?). This campaign didn't create my connection to the district. It came from it.

What She Believes.

I believe in an America where if you work full-time, you can afford to live. Where your kids get a well-funded education regardless of your zip code. Where getting sick doesn't mean going bankrupt. Where the government works for people, not corporations.

I'm raising my kids to understand the true history of this country, to find passion, to appreciate past hardships, and to believe that standing up for others should come from absolute kindness, not obligation. That's the kind of America I want to fight for in Congress.
